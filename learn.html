CalOSHA Guidelines

Information based on guidelines found from CalOSHA website, found here

Here are the CalOSHA guidelines for any outdoor workers at all temperatures:

Employers must provide their employees with clean, fresh, free drinking water. It must be at least one quarter per hour of work.

Shade must be available whether the temperature is above 80 degrees F or under. Anytime an employee feels they are overheating, they should be allowed and encouraged to take a preventative cool down in the shade.

Here are the CalOSHA guidelines when the temperature is 80 degrees F or higher:

In addition to the baseline requirements outlined above, when the temperature is 80 degrees F or higher, additional precautions must be taken.

The amount of shade provided must be enough so that all employees have enough space to sit in a normal posture and not be touching.

Additionally, employees must be monitored by a supervisor for heat related illness in this temperature range

Here are the CalOSHA guidelines when the temperature is 95 degrees F and over:

In addition to the baseline guidelines and 80 degree F and higher guidelines, when temperature is 95 F or higher, high heat procedures must be enacted.

Employees must have communication access to supervisors at all times

Supervisors must observe employees for any signs of heat illness

If a supervisor has more than 20 employees, there must be a mandatory buddy system

If an employee is working alone, communication by radio or cell phone must be a regular occurence

Employees must be reminded to drink plenty of water

If the employee is in the agriculture industry, the employee must take a ten minute mandatory cool down period every 2 hours.
